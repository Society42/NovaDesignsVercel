Asessment

Scene Safety Assessment


Prioritise your safety and that of your team. Assess the scene for any potential hazards such as traffic, fire, or violence.

Establish safe access to the patient while maintaining awareness of surroundings.

Initial Patient Assessment:


Conduct a rapid initial assessment to determine the patient's level of consciousness, airway patency, breathing, and circulation.

Address any life-threatening conditions immediately.

Primary Survey


Danger: Check for any surrounding danger around the casualty, if there is any danger or something that poses a risk to you or another person attempt to remove it safely.


Response: Check to see if the casualty is responding, this can be done by shaking their shoulders and asking them to open their eyes or asking them simple questions like “What year is it.”.


Airways: Ensure that the casualty’s airways are clear if they are unconscious, this may involve tilting their head back using 2 fingers and tilting their chin back and one hand on the forehead doing the same tilting back motion. If they are conscious then you may ask them to sit up, if they are unable to do so continue with DRABC.


Breathing: Once the casualty's airways are clear check to see if they are breathing clearly. This can be done by putting your ear to their mouth and listening for breathing for up to 10 seconds and watching for a rising and falling movement of their chest. Note there is a sign of cardiac arrest known as “agonal gasping” which is when They can open their mouths, extend their neck, and take usually slow, sometimes noisy gasps.


Circulation: When you have completed all other previous steps you must then check for any severe bleeding. If they have severe bleeding you must treat such accordingly if they are conscious or unconscious.


Secondary Survey


Allergy: Check to see if the person has anything which displays they have an allergy or ask somebody who knows the casualty about any alleries.


Medication: Check to see if the casualty takes any medication


Previous Medical History: Check to see if the patient has any previous medical history by looking for medical jewellery or asking people who know the casualty.


Last Meal: Try and find out when the patient had their last meal.


Event History: Find out if the incident is due to an illness or an accident.


Normal Vital Signs


Heart Rate (Pulse): 60-100 beats per minute


Respiratory Rate: 12-20 breaths per minute


Blood Pressure: 90/60 mmHg to 120/80 mmHg


Temperature: 97.8°F to 99.1°F | 36.5°C to 37.3°C
