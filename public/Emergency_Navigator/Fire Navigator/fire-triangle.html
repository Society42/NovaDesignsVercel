What is the Fire Triangle?

The fire triangle is a simple model for understanding the necessary ingredients for most fires. It consists of three elements: Heat, Fuel, and Oxygen. All three components must be present for a fire to ignite and sustain itself.

Components of the Fire Triangle

  • Heat: The energy source that raises the material to its ignition temperature.
  • Fuel: Any combustible material that can ignite, such as wood, paper, or gasoline.
  • Oxygen: Necessary for combustion, typically requires at least 16% oxygen to sustain a fire.

Eliminating One Component

To extinguish a fire, one of the three elements must be removed. For example, applying water removes heat, while smothering a fire removes oxygen.
