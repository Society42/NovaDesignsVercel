Call Grades

  • I Grade
  • S Grade
  • E Grade
  • R Grade

Immediate

(I) grade - I-grade calls refer to situations where the presence of a police officer can substantially influence the outcome of an incident. These incidents typically involve potential danger to life, serious threats of violence, significant property damage, or severe injury. Response to such urgent calls is required within 15 minutes.

Significant

(S) grade - S-grade calls are incidents where there is a level of importance or urgency requiring initial police action, but without the immediate need for an emergency response. These situations may involve significant circumstances that necessitate police attention within 60 minutes.

Extended

(E) grade - E-grade calls are incidents where police presence is necessary, although an emergency response is not immediate. Such situations require police attention within 48 hours.

Referred

(R) grade - R-grade calls indicate situations where police presence is unnecessary, indicating that the caller has been appropriately assisted by the call handler.
