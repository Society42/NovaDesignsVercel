About the FBI

The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) is the United States' premier federal law enforcement agency. Founded in 1908, the FBI investigates a wide range of criminal activities, including terrorism, cybercrime, organized crime, and civil rights violations. The agency's mission is to uphold and enforce federal laws, protect the United States from national security threats, and ensure the safety and well-being of the American public.

We are the most technologically advanced department in North Carolina Roleplay, utilizing cutting-edge tools and techniques to protect and serve our community. Our highly trained personnel work together to ensure the safety and security of all citizens within the United States, keeping the streets safe from criminal activity and fostering a strong community.

However, it is important to note that we do not associate with the real-life Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI). Our operations are confined to North Carolina Roleplay, where we focus on creating realistic, immersive experiences for our members.

Divisions

Hostage Rescue Team (H.R.T)

HRT Unit - Critical Incident Response Group

The Critical Incident Response Group (CIRG) is a specialized team within the Hostage Rescue Team, trained to respond rapidly to critical incidents, including hostage situations, active shooter scenarios, and high-risk rescues. The unit provides rapid tactical response and supports law enforcement agencies in emergency situations requiring advanced tactical capabilities.

HRT Unit - Bomb Technicians / Bomb Squad

The Bomb Technicians, often part of the Bomb Squad, are specialized in the detection, disarmament, and safe disposal of explosives. The team is highly trained to handle explosive devices found at crime scenes, terrorist threats, or in hostage rescue operations. Their expertise is crucial in ensuring the safety of both responders and civilians during high-risk incidents involving explosives.

HRT Unit - Joint Terrorism Task Force

The Joint Terrorism Task Force (JTTF) is a collaborative effort between various federal, state, and local law enforcement agencies to prevent and respond to terrorist threats. HRT members working within the JTTF are trained to deal with terrorism-related incidents, counter-terrorism operations, and threats to national security. This division focuses on intelligence gathering, surveillance, and tactical response to terrorism-related situations.

HRT Unit - National Security Service

The National Security Service (NSS) within the HRT is tasked with handling incidents that pose a significant risk to national security, including intelligence operations and responses to high-profile threats. This unit works closely with other federal agencies and the military to address situations such as cyber threats, foreign espionage, and other national security risks that require a swift and coordinated tactical response.

Cyber Crime Division (C.C.D.)

CCD Unit - Joint Terrorism Task Force

The Joint Terrorism Task Force (JTTF) is a collaborative initiative between federal, state, and local law enforcement agencies focused on combating terrorism and protecting national security. The CCD unit works within the JTTF to investigate and prevent cyberterrorism, online extremism, and other cyber threats related to terrorism.

CCD Unit - National Security Service

The National Security Service (NSS) is tasked with protecting the nation's infrastructure from cyber threats. The CCD unit within the NSS focuses on investigating cyber espionage, foreign cyber attacks, and other activities that compromise national security through digital means. It works closely with government agencies to safeguard sensitive data and prevent cyber attacks on critical systems.

CCD Unit - Evidence Response Team

The Evidence Response Team (ERT) is responsible for collecting and preserving digital evidence in cybercrime investigations. This unit is specialized in securing and analyzing electronic data from devices such as computers, mobile phones, and servers. The team ensures that evidence is handled with care and integrity, supporting investigations in cases such as data breaches, online fraud, and other cybercrime activities.

FBI K9 Unit

Bomb Detection K9s

These canines are trained to detect explosive materials, assisting in bomb sweeps for public events, airports, and crime scenes. They play a crucial role in preventing bomb threats and saving lives.

Narcotics Detection K9s

These dogs are specialized in detecting illegal drugs and narcotics. They assist in raids, drug trafficking investigations, and criminal operations, playing a key role in preventing the spread of illicit drugs.

Patrol K9s

Patrol K9s assist in general law enforcement duties, including suspect apprehension, crowd control, and searches for illegal substances. They are essential in maintaining public safety in high-risk areas.

Recruitment Office

Interview Staff

The Interview Staff are responsible for conducting interviews with prospective candidates for various FBI positions. They assess applicants' qualifications, skills, and suitability for law enforcement roles. Their role includes reviewing resumes, administering psychological evaluations, and ensuring that candidates meet the agency's strict criteria for service. The Interview Staff also guide applicants through the recruitment process and ensure that they understand the expectations and responsibilities of joining the FBI.

National Internal Affairs

Internal Affairs

The Internal Affairs (IA) division is responsible for investigating allegations of misconduct, corruption, and unethical behavior within the FBI. IA conducts thorough inquiries into complaints against FBI personnel and works to maintain the integrity and professionalism of the agency. They play a critical role in ensuring that all agents and staff adhere to the highest standards of ethical conduct and that the public trust in the agency remains intact. The division also works to prevent internal corruption and take corrective action when necessary.
