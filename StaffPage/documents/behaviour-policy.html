Conduct and Professionalism

A. Code of Ethics

FBI agents are expected to follow the highest ethical standards.

  • Integrity: Always act truthfully and transparently in all matters.
  • Respect: Treat all individuals with dignity, regardless of their status.
  • Accountability: Acknowledge mistakes and take responsibility for actions.
  • Impartiality: Treat all people fairly, free of bias or prejudice.
  • Courage: Stand by the law, even when facing personal or professional challenges.

B. Role of the FBI Agent

The role of an FBI agent is to uphold federal laws and protect national security.

  • Enforcing laws regarding national security, criminal activities, and civil rights.
  • Investigating criminal enterprises, terrorism, organized crime, and other threats.
  • Gathering intelligence and providing support to national and international law enforcement agencies.

Please note roles change depending on division, role, and assigned roles.

Chain of Custody and Evidence Handling

A. Importance of Chain of Custody

  • Evidence must be preserved from the moment it is collected until it is presented in court. This ensures its authenticity and integrity.

B. Procedures for Handling Evidence

  • Collection: Use appropriate tools to collect evidence with minimal contact (e.g., gloves, tweezers). Record key details like time, date, and location.
    Example: For a firearm, use gloves to place it in a secure, sealed evidence bag, noting serial number and location found.
  • Packaging and Labeling: Place evidence in suitable containers to prevent contamination or damage. Label each item with the case number, description, and collector's name.
    Example: A knife should be placed in a rigid box, sealed, and labeled with the necessary case info.
  • Transportation: Transport evidence securely, ensuring it's properly sealed and documented to avoid tampering or loss.
    Example: A sealed bag containing drugs should be transported in a locked container with transfer records.
