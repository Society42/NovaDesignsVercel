FBI HANDBOOK

Confidentiality Notice: Do not leak any information from this handbook. Violation of this rule will result in disciplinary action, including possible termination from the Federal Bureau of Investigation and prosecution under the Espionage Act (USC 798).

Leaking information refers to the unauthorized disclosure or release of sensitive, confidential, or classified information to individuals, organizations, or the public without proper clearance. In the context of the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), this includes any intentional or unintentional sharing of materials, data, or intelligence that is protected by federal law, regulations, or agency policy.

Introduction

The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) is committed to maintaining a professional, respectful, and inclusive environment for all personnel and collaborators. This Behavior Policy outlines the expectations for staff conduct and the disciplinary actions that may result from violations.

High Command or the NIAO may escalate any case directly to severe disciplinary actions, including immediate termination, if deemed necessary for the integrity of the FBI or national security.

Expected Behavior

A. Professionalism and Respect

FBI personnel must treat colleagues, community members, and partners with respect, courtesy, and professionalism at all times. Discriminatory, harassing, or offensive behavior based on race, gender, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or any other protected characteristic is strictly prohibited and will not be tolerated.

B. Integrity and Honesty

Honesty and integrity are core FBI values. Personnel must always be truthful and transparent in their actions and communications. Plagiarism, dishonesty, or any form of deceit is unacceptable and will result in disciplinary action.

C. Confidentiality

FBI personnel must protect the privacy and confidentiality of classified information, operational details, and sensitive data. Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information will lead to immediate disciplinary action and may result in termination or legal consequences.

D. Responsibility and Accountability

All personnel are accountable for their actions and decisions. They must take ownership of their duties and address any mistakes promptly. Failure to meet responsibilities or negligence in fulfilling duties will result in disciplinary measures.

E. Collaboration and Teamwork

Effective collaboration is essential to the success of FBI operations. Personnel are expected to communicate openly, share relevant information, and support team members. Sabotaging, undermining, or failing to cooperate with colleagues will not be tolerated.

F. Communication

All internal and external communications must be respectful, professional, and constructive. Use of inappropriate language, aggressive behavior, or any form of disrespectful communication will result in disciplinary action.

G. Conflict Resolution

Conflicts should be addressed promptly and constructively through open dialogue. If resolution cannot be achieved at the team level, escalation to higher management is encouraged.

Prohibited Behavior

A. Harassment and Bullying

Any form of harassment, bullying, or intimidation—whether verbal, physical, or written—is strictly prohibited. This includes unwanted advances, threats, derogatory comments, or any behavior that creates a hostile environment.

B. Discrimination

Discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or any other protected characteristic is prohibited. Equal treatment and opportunities must be ensured for all personnel and collaborators.

C. Violence and Threats

Any form of violence or threats of violence, including physical altercations or verbal threats, is strictly prohibited. Such behavior will result in immediate disciplinary action, including potential termination.

D. Substance Abuse

The use, possession, or distribution of illegal drugs or alcohol while on duty is strictly prohibited. Substance abuse will result in disciplinary action and legal action.

F. Insubordination

Refusal to follow lawful instructions from supervisors or engaging in defiant behavior constitutes insubordination. Such actions will result in disciplinary measures.

Disciplinary Actions

A. Types of Violations

Minor Violations

Examples: Unprofessional conduct, such as inappropriate language or minor rudeness. Failure to adhere to the dress code or tardiness. Minor procedural errors, such as misfiled reports.

Potential Consequences: Verbal Warning (sometimes documented in personnel file). Mandatory retraining or coaching. Accumulation of strikes for repeated offenses.

Major Violations

Examples: Misuse of authority or resources. Corruption or conflict of interest. Excessive force or violations of civil rights.

Potential Consequences: Immediate suspension, pending investigation. Issuance of strikes or termination. Legal review and potential criminal charges.

Illegal Activities

Examples: Theft, bribery, or embezzlement. Espionage or leaking classified information. Participation in organized crime or acts of terrorism.

Potential Consequences: Immediate termination. Referral for criminal prosecution, including federal felony charges. Imprisonment if convicted.

B. Reporting Misconduct

Internal Reporting

Violations or suspected misconduct must be reported to a direct supervisor or the National Internal Affairs Office (NIAO). You have the option to stay anonymous for sensitive cases.

Whistleblower Protection

Employees who report misconduct in good faith are fully protected from retaliation under federal law, including the Whistleblower Protection Act of 1989. Retaliation against whistleblowers will result in severe disciplinary measures, including termination of the retaliating party.

C. Disciplinary Actions and Strike System

Strike System

A formal strike system ensures consistent consequences for repeat offenders while allowing flexibility for unique circumstances:

  • First Strike: Written warning and/or mandatory retraining.
  • Second Strike: Suspension without pay for 3-7 days, depending on severity.
  • Third Strike: Immediate termination or escalation to criminal charges if applicable.

Escalation Based on Role or Severity

Personnel in high-command roles or those with access to sensitive information will face stricter evaluations. A minor violation by high-command personnel may be escalated to a Major Violation due to the potential impact on the FBI’s reputation or operations.

Punishments by Violation Severity

Minor Violations: Verbal or written warnings. Retraining or reassignment of duties.

Major Violations: Suspension. Demotion or reassignment to non-sensitive roles. Termination, if warranted.

Illegal Activities: Immediate dismissal. Arrest and referral for prosecution under federal law. For espionage or severe breaches, potential life imprisonment or other statutory penalties.

High Command/NIAO Authority

High Command and the National Internal Affairs Office reserve the right to escalate disciplinary actions based on the circumstances. This includes bypassing the strike system when deemed necessary for the protection of FBI operations, public trust, or national security.

Conclusion

The FBI is committed to maintaining the highest standards of conduct, professionalism, and legality. Every agent must carry out their duties with integrity, fairness, and respect for the law and civil rights. Follow the protocols outlined in this handbook, stay informed, and always act in the best interest of justice.
