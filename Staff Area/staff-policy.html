Emergency Navigator Staff Policy

Purpose: The purpose of this policy is to establish clear guidelines and expectations for all staff members of Emergency Navigator. Our goal is to ensure a professional, respectful, and efficient working environment that fosters growth and supports the community's needs.

General Conduct

  • Professionalism: Staff members must conduct themselves professionally at all times, being respectful, courteous, and maintaining a positive attitude.
  • Confidentiality: Staff must respect the privacy of community members and keep sensitive information confidential. Breaches will lead to disciplinary action.
  • Conflict of Interest: Avoid any activities that conflict with responsibilities to Emergency Navigator. Potential conflicts must be disclosed to management.
  • Integrity: Honesty and integrity are paramount. Staff must uphold high ethical standards and protect Emergency Navigator's reputation.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Adherence to Role Descriptions: Understand and fulfill duties outlined in the role descriptions.
  • Collaboration: Communicate openly and work effectively with other team members.
  • Accountability: Staff members are responsible for their actions, meeting deadlines, attending meetings, and following through on commitments.
  • Continuous Improvement: Seek opportunities for professional development and offer suggestions for improvements.

Communication

  • Open Communication: Maintain open lines of communication with supervisors and team members.
  • Respectful Discourse: Communication should be respectful and constructive. Harassment or bullying will not be tolerated.
  • Response Time: Respond to messages and inquiries from the community and team within 24 hours.

Performance and Evaluation

  • Performance Reviews: Regular performance reviews will assess contributions, provide feedback, and set goals for future development.
  • Goal Setting: Set personal and professional goals and work towards achieving them with supervisorsâ€™ support.
  • Feedback Mechanisms: Be open to receiving and giving constructive feedback to peers and supervisors.

Disciplinary Actions

  • Progressive Discipline: Progressive discipline will apply to staff who violate policies or performance standards, starting with verbal warnings and escalating to suspension or termination if needed.
  • Immediate Termination: Gross misconduct, harassment, or breach of confidentiality may lead to immediate termination without prior warnings.
  • Appeals: Staff may appeal disciplinary actions in writing within seven days to senior management.

Leave and Absences

  • Leave Requests: Submit leave requests in advance whenever possible. Emergency leave should be communicated as soon as possible.
  • Absence Reporting: Unplanned absences must be reported to the immediate supervisor at the earliest opportunity.
  • Coverage Arrangements: Staff members are responsible for arranging coverage for their duties during planned absences.

Respect & Safety

  • Safe Environment: Emergency Navigator is committed to providing a safe and healthy working environment.
  • Mental Health & Support: Staff members are encouraged to seek support when needed. Resources are available to all staff members.

Conclusion

This policy ensures that staff members of Emergency Navigator work harmoniously, ethically, and effectively. Adherence is essential for maintaining the integrity and success of our organisation.

By accepting a position with Emergency Navigator, all staff members agree to abide by this policy and contribute positively to our community.
