Routes of Administration

  • Intramuscular (IM): You directly inject medication into a patient’s muscular tissue. Doing this may make the medication work a little slower.
  • Intranasally (IN): By using a mist with a syringe with a tip on it to squirt medication into a patient’s nose. The most common medication for this is Narcan.
  • Intravenous (IV): Injecting the medication directly into the patient’s veins is one of the quickest ways for medication to take effect.
  • Intraosseous (IO): By drilling directly into the bone, the patient receives medication directly to the bone marrow. It is another fast way for medication to begin working on a patient.
  • Sublingual (SL): By putting the medication under the tongue to dissolve.

Drugs

  • Adenosine: Adenosine exerts its effects by decreasing conduction through the AV node, slowing the heart rate during instances of tachycardia.
    Route: Given via IV
    Dosage: (12mg IV, followed by 10ml NS flush)
  • Aspirin: Anti-inflammatory drug that blocks the clotting factor of the blood, it can be used in chest pains as well as other inflammation.
    Route: Administered orally as a tablet
    Dosage: (81mg/tablet, repeatable x4)
  • Amiodarone: This can be used on patients in V-Fib and V-Tach, as it steadies and slows the electrical impulses on the heart and thus helps regulate them.
    Route: Given via IV or IO
    Dosage: (V-Fib/Pulseless VTach - 300mg IV, repeat doses of 150mg for recurrent)(V-Tach with pulse - 150mgIV over 10 min)
  • Atropine: It’s an anticholinergic that increases heart rate and decreases gastrointestinal secretions. Used when a PT has bradycardia as well as low blood pressure secondary to bradycardia and organophosphate poisoning.
    Route: Given via IV or IO
    Dosage: (Bradycardia: 0.5mg repeated every 3-5 min (maximum dose 3mg) (Cardiac Arrest: 1mg repeated up to 3 times)
  • Benadryl: Can be used with minor allergic reactions as well as early anaphylaxis. It is an antihistamine.
    Route: Administered via EMTB - PO/SL, AEMT - IV, IM, IO
    Dosage: (25-50mg)
  • Cardizem: A calcium channel blocker designed to relax vessel stress, decrease heart rate and blood pressure and decrease the load on the cardiac muscles. This medication is administered by attaching the vial of cardizem powder to the cardizem IV bag and shake to mix, then connect IV line to PT and administer. This should be given to PTs with unstable tachycardia, atrial fibrillation, atrial flutter and chest pain as a result of angina and NOT myocardial infarction (STEMI).
    Route: Given via IV, IO
    Dosage: (20mg IV/IO)
  • Dextrose (D10):Given to patients who are in a hypoglycemic shock (blood sugar level lower than 60 mg/dl). Not to be used in infiltrated IV sites due to tissue necrosis. Use caution in areas like swamps or rivers, mountainous environments, or low-hygiene areas that you may encounter.
    Route: May also be given through IO. Given via IV.
    Dosage: (D10 IV / IO titrated to effect blood glucose level ≥70)
  • Glucagon: Medicine used to treat severe hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) in diabetes patients treated with insulin who have passed out or cannot take some form of sugar by mouth.
    Route: Given via IM
    Dosage: (1-2mg repeatable 1x)
  • Epinephrine/Adrenaline: Comes in different concentrations and these concentrations can be used for different things.
    Warning: Do not administer to PTs with elevated heart rates, pregnant PTs or PTs with a history of heart disease.
    Route: Given via IM, Nebuliser, IV, IO. Please read dosage for what dosages you should use for each route.
    Dosage: 0.5mg: for bronchodilation (opens up the lungs a bit). For severe asthma, severe allergic reactions, anaphylactic shock. Given via IM or nebulizer.

    1mg: treatment and prophylaxis of cardiac arrest. Given via IV or IO.
  • Fentanyl: Narcotic, depressant that decreases sensitivity to pain and vasodilates (drops blood pressure).
    Warning: Withhold administration if SBP is <100. Do not give to PTs with respiratory depression or distress.
    Route: Given via IV or IO.
    Dosage: Normal adult dose = 50mcg, may repeat once (max 100mcg).
  • Etomidate: Sedative used for Rapid Sequence Intubation.
    Route: Given via IV or IO.
    Dosage: Usual adult dose = 20mg.
  • Ketamine: Dissociative Anesthetic that can be used for sedation as well as pain management. This drug will make a PT hallucinate and hysteric.
    Warning: Do not administer if severe hypertension is present, it will raise blood pressure. Do not give to PTs with head injuries, history of drug abuse, intoxication, high blood pressure.
    Route: Given via IV, IO, IM.
    Dosage:
    • Normal adult dose (Sedation): 2mg/kg IV/IO, rough dose 120mg, Max 200mg.
    • Normal adult dose (Pain Management): 20mg IV over 1 minute, repeat 1x.
    • Normal adult dose (Agitation/Delirium): 3-5mg IM.
  • Ketorolac/Toradol: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory used to manage pain.
    Warning: Do not administer to PTs with major trauma, renal failure, the elderly, or those who have experienced adverse reactions to NSAIDs.
    Route: Administered via IM/IV.
    Dosage: One dose of 30mg IM / 15mg IV.
  • Lactated Ringer (LR) solution: Primary intravenous fluid used to rehydrate dehydrated PTs, trauma PTs experiencing blood loss, burn victims, etc. This solution contains more electrolytes and nutrients than regular saline (NS) and will rehydrate and maintain a healthy nutrient level.
    Warning: Giving too much fluid to a PT with internal or external bleeding can result in flooding with too much fluid and not enough blood. Therefore, keep the blood pressure stable at 80-90 systolic.
    Route: Administered via IV.
    Dosage: Available in 250ml, 500ml, 1,000ml bags.
  • Lidocaine: Anesthetic that can be used to reduce the speed of electrical impulses in the heart, slowing down the PT’s heart. Typically given for refractory V-Fib.
    Warning: Do not administer if PTs have WPWs on monitor, heart blocks noted on monitor, or a pre-diagnosed decreased blood potassium level.
    Route: Administered via IV/IO.
    Dosage:
    • For refractory V-Fib: Administer as per protocols for specific conditions.
    • For pain control at the IO site: 2mg of 2% Lidocaine can be administered x2 over 2 minutes (2/2/2/2) through IO.
  • Morphine: Narcotic that can be used to reduce pain. Will cause low blood pressure as well as a low pulse.
    Warning: Do not give to PTs with respiratory depression or distress. Once Narcan is administered, this will stop working.
    Route: Given via IV or IO.
    Dosage: Normal adult dose = 5mg, may repeat once (max 10mg).
  • Naloxone / Narcan: A medication that temporarily negates the effects of opioids.
    Route: Given via IV or IN.
    Dosage:
    • IV: 0.4mg to 2mg, may repeat every 2-3 minutes as needed.
    • IN: 4mg, may repeat every 2-3 minutes as needed.
  • Ibuprofen (Advil): Medication given to patients experiencing small, acute levels of pain. While this drug won’t thin your blood like many other medications, it will still slow down clotting time for active bleeds.
    Warning: May slow down clotting time for active bleeds.
    Route: Given via PO.
    Dosage: Normal adult dose = 400-600mg, no more than 800mg.
  • Nitroglycerine: Used for chest pain of cardiac origin when the PT’s systolic BP is above 180.
    Warning: Do not give to PTs who have a known sensitivity to nitrate medications, have taken erectile dysfunction medications in the past 24h (e.g., Viagra, Cialis, Levitra, Stendra, Staxyn, Sildenafil, Avanafil, Tadalafil, Vardenafil), or are hypotensive (typically <90 systolic BP).
    Route:
    • SL (EMT-B)
    • Transdermal paste (AEMT)
    • IV/IO (EMT-P)
    Dosage: Administer 0.4mg as soon as indicated.
  • Norepinephrine: Used for hypotension, particularly in shock after an IV bolus is established. It helps maintain heart function and manage blood pressure by counteracting the effects of adrenaline.
    Route: Given via IV or IO, accompanying the IV bolus (dual arm saline).
    Dosage: Normal adult dose = 0.3 mcg/kg/min. For an average 73kg adult, this is approximately 48mcg/min.
  • Sodium Bicarbonate: Used in late cardiac arrest to combine with acids and increase pH. Also useful in cases of severe acidosis due to hyperventilation and for PTs with suspected crush trauma.
    Route: Given via IV or IO.
    Dosage: Initial bolus of 50 mEq.
  • Succinylcholine: Skeletal muscle relaxant used for intubation; paralyzes skeletal muscles including respiratory muscles.
    Route: Given via IV or IO.
    Dosage: 1.0 mg/kg. For an average 73kg adult, this is approximately 73mg.
  • Tranexamic Acid (TXA): Antifibrinolytic that promotes blood clots and helps control bleeding in severe hemorrhage PTs. Note: Before administering TXA for external bleeding, attempt pressure with sterile dressing, TQs, etc., and consider transport time to the nearest hospital.
    Route: Given via IV or IO.
    Dosage: 1g as soon as indicated.
  • Tylenol (Paracetamol): A pain reliever that reduces pain or fever. Should not be used if the PT has any liver disease.
    Warning: Should not be used if the PT has any liver disease.
    Route: Usually taken orally.
    Dosage: Adult dose = 1000mg PO.
  • Versed/Midazolam: Benzodiazepine used for sedation, seizure management, and in small doses for pain management. This drug will make a PT drowsy and out of it.
    Warning: Do not give to PTs with a history of drug abuse, kidney failure, heart failure, decreased lung function, or decreased blood flow to extremities.
    Route: Given via IV, IO, IM, or IN.
    Dosage: Normal adult dose = 2.5mg.
  • Zofran/Ondansetron: Used for relief of nausea or vomiting.
    Warning: Do not give if the PT takes apomorphine (due to risk of severe hypotension) or if the PT has known hypersensitivity to ondansetron.
    Route: Given via IV, IO, or IM.
    Dosage: Normal adult dose = 4mg over 2-5 minutes, may repeat once.
  • Cyanokit: Used to treat smoke inhalation.
    Warning: Substantial changes in blood pressure may occur; monitor BP closely.
    Route: Administered via IV or IO.
    Dosage: 5g over 15 minutes.
  • Labetalol: Used to treat hypertensive emergencies.
    Warning: Do not administer to PTs with severe bradycardia or a history of prolonged hypotension.
    Route: Administered via IV.
    Dosage: Initial push of 10-20mg.
