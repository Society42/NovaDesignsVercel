Call Responces

Category 1


For life-threatening injuries and illnesses, specifically cardiac arrest, the response time will need to be an average of seven minutes.

Category 2


For emergency calls, such as those for stroke patients, the response time will need to be an average of 18 minutes.

Category 3


For urgent calls, such as those for abdominal pains, which may include patients being treated in their own homes, the response time will need to be an average of 120 minutes.

Category 4


Less urgent calls, such as those for diarrhea and vomiting or back pain, will often result in patients being given advice over the telephone or referred to another service, such as a GP or pharmacist. These less urgent calls will be responded to at least nine out of ten times within 180 minutes.
