Fire Classes

Class A Fire

Class A fire involves ordinary combustible materials such as wood, paper, cloth, and plastics.

Class B Fire

Class B fire involves flammable liquids such as oil and grease.

Class C Fire

Class C fire involves gases such as gasoline and solvents.

Class D Fire

Class D fire involves combustible metals such as magnesium, titanium, potassium, and sodium.

Class F Fire

Class F fire involves cooking oils and fats, typically found in commercial kitchens.

Electrical Fire

An electrical fire involves energized electrical equipment or appliances, where the primary hazard is the potential for electrical shock.
