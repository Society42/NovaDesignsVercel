Asessment

Scene Safety Assessment


Prioritise your safety and that of your team. Assess the scene for any potential hazards such as traffic, fire, or violence.

Establish safe access to the patient while maintaining awareness of surroundings.

Initial Patient Assessment:


Conduct a rapid initial assessment to determine the patient's level of consciousness, airway patency, breathing, and circulation.

Address any life-threatening conditions immediately.

Primary Survey


Airway: Ensure the patient's airway is open and clear. Use appropriate maneuvers or equipment if needed.


Breathing: Assess breathing rate, depth, and quality. Administer oxygen if necessary.


Circulation: Check for signs of circulation such as pulse and skin color. Control any life-threatening bleeding.


Secondary Survey


Head-to-Toe Assessment: Perform a systematic assessment of the patient's head, neck, chest, abdomen, pelvis, extremities, and back. Identify any additional injuries or medical conditions.


Vital Signs: Obtain baseline vital signs including heart rate, blood pressure, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation. Monitor vital signs throughout the assessment and treatment process.
